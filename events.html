Undergraduate Colloquia

What is it?

The undergraduate colloquia are like the usual physics department colloquia, however these talks are by undergrads for undergrads. Like the usual colloquia, the presentations could be talks on some interesting area of physics research, but they can also be used as a venue to practice a talk before a conference or get feedback on your thesis poster or practice a presentation for a class. Basically this is an opportunity for undergraduates to practice their presentation skills. To find the colloquia and presentation times click here or go to the calendar.

Calendar

Can I participate?

Everyone is welcome (and encouraged!) to attend the colloquia. They will be accessible to undergrads and give an opportunity to learn new things about interesting research problems in physics.

If you would like to give a presentation, send us an email. Please include your name and "undergraduate colloquium" in the subject line and the date you'd like to present, the title and abstract for your presentation in the body of the email. You can give a presentation in any format (talk, poster video) and it can be any length less than 50 minutes (we could possibly coordinate multiple short presentations to happen on the same day). Use the colloquia as an opportunity to practice presentations for classes or conferences or just to tell other undergrads about some cool physics.
