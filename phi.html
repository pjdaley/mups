Physics Help Initiative

What is it?

The Physics Help Initiative (PHI) is a free student-led help initiative for first year students taking physics courses. Upper year physics students are available for help during regular hours. We can give you help with homework problems, test and exam preparation and all your physics related questions. Jasper Grond and Katelyn Dixon are the PHI coordinators for Summer 2015.

First Year Physics: Tips and Tricks

Go To Class

Attending the lectures is a great to keeping up with the material. Falling behind is the single worst thing you can do in any class, especially physics.

Read Textbook Section before Lecture

Reading the textbook first will help familiarize you with the content so that you can follow the lecture more easily. If it helps you, make notes while you read. Be sure to ask yourself questions as you read to check your understanding, for example when a claim is made you may ask "How do I know that is true?" or when a new concept is introduced you may ask yourself "Is this similar to a concept I learned previously?" or "How does this relate to the other concepts I have learned?". You may not fully grasp everything you read, however you can go to lecture to clear up anything you did not quite understand.

Understand don't Memorize

When something you read or hear in lecture doe not make sense, ask a T.A. or professor or peer about it and try to understand the answer. Simply memorizing Newton's laws and formulas for kinematics, momentum, energy, etc. does not answer physics problems, understanding the underlying concepts and knowing how to apply them does.

Practice a Little Often

This is true about learning anything, by lecture notes on a recent concept and trying a few practice problems frequently (say three times a week or more) will ensure that you are consistently developing the neural pathways relating to that concept and keep you on top of the material. If you need help understanding the concept then just ask someone and reflect on the answer and try more problems independently to check your understanding.
